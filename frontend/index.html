What you'll need

What is tumor sequencing?

Tumor sequencing reads the DNA of cancer cells to find mutations — changes that make the tumor different from normal tissue. These mutations can be used to design a vaccine that teaches the immune system to recognize and attack the cancer.

What you'll get: A VCF file (Variant Call Format) listing all the mutations found. This is what you'll upload in the next step.

Where can I get sequencing done?

Ask your veterinary oncologist about tumor sequencing options. Several services offer this:

  • FidoCure — Veterinary precision medicine, works directly with vet clinics
  • Antech / IDEXX — Major veterinary diagnostics labs
  • University veterinary hospitals — Many offer sequencing through their oncology departments

Typical cost: $800–$2,500 depending on the type of sequencing and provider.

Turnaround: 2–4 weeks from biopsy to results.

How does a personalized cancer vaccine work?

Every tumor has unique mutations. Some of these mutations create altered proteins that the immune system can learn to recognize. A personalized vaccine uses these unique protein fragments (called "vaccine targets") to train the immune system to find and destroy cancer cells.

This is the same approach used in some human cancer treatments and is being explored in veterinary oncology.
